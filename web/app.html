About Me

Founder of @RentChek. Lead Software Engineer at @InVisionApp. Coding enthusiast. Husband & Father.

I've been programming professionally since 2007 and enthusiast since my high school years. When I was first introduced to my IBM Intel Pentium II 300Mhz I quickly developed an unquenchable thirst for information technology that has never left me. My first taste of real programmatic thinking came when I discovered a little tool for developing role playing games called RPG Maker. At the age of 16 I developed my first RPG game called Time Trigger which was a great hit. For entertainment I found myself setting up dedicated servers for hosting Counter-Strike games which eventually led me to developing websites for our local clan. At the time, websites were only just starting to gain the ability to actually be an application. Once I realized this, I quickly noticed the potential for web application development and that's where I took my career when I started work with Statusfirm.

At Statusfirm I worked on media websites that served 1000's of simultaneous viewers, but this didn't quite satisfy my application development desire. I ended up finding Bluedrop where I started with a couple of other developers to build the next eLearning platform. This is where CoursePark was born. It is really quite the experience to see something you developed straight from line 1 to being used by 10's of thousands of users everyday.

My programming journey took me to InVision next. During my tenure there, I made impactful contributions across diverse projects, primarily centered around enhancing product quality and efficiency. I optimized developer experiences using Docker, reducing setup times significantly. This extended to revolutionizing software delivery at the company through improved build pipelines, Kubernetes and a custom Slack bot, empowering frequent releases and isolated rollbacks. I led the migration of a vital service containing 300+ million records from MySQL to DynamoDB seamlessly. Stepping up to an Interim Engineering Manager role, I balanced leadership while maintaining my coding passion. I championed the integration of AWS, Docker, and CI/CD for over 100 microservices, improving velocity and ensuring stability. My lessons learned from post-mortems continue to guide my engineering decisions.

Off the clock, I'm always experimenting with new technologies so I can remain on top of the curve, which is how RentChek and Synapsie came to be. My latest endeavour has been iOS application development which has been a great learning experience coming from the web development perspective.

Outside my home office, my loving wife and two beautiful children complete my life with joy & happiness and fuel my determination to be the best version of myself, both at home and at work.

Enhancing Developer Experience and Software Delivery

At the outset, I tackled the challenge of suboptimal developer experiences. I optimized efficiency by leveraging Docker to transform projects and services into manageable and reliable entities. This initiative drastically reduced setup times, empowering developers to establish working environments in mere minutes, a significant leap from the previously cumbersome hours.

This endeavor naturally extended into revolutionizing our software delivery process. Collaborating with a dedicated team, I harnessed Kubernetes and streamlined deployment techniques which included building a custom Slack bot to improve ease and transparency of releases. By orchestrating Docker images for each service, we empowered developers to execute multiple independent releases daily, while also facilitating isolated rollbacks, leading to a substantial boost in engineering velocity.

Empowering Backend Services

Transitioning into a backend-focused role, I spearheaded the transformation of the Assets API service—a pivotal piece managing over 300 million user-uploaded assets. Migrating this service from MySQL to DynamoDB was a formidable challenge, executed seamlessly through meticulous planning to ensure zero downtime and data loss.

Leadership and Revitalization

Stepping up during a reorganization, I assumed the role of Interim Engineering Manager. I conducted performance reviews, facilitated team coordination, and collaborated with stakeholders to ensure seamless planning and execution of work. Despite this managerial role, my passion for coding eventually led me back to my role as a Lead Software Engineer.

In the later stages, I undertook a significant project aimed at revitalizing our local development environment. Through the integration of AWS, Docker, continuous integration, and custom-built CLIs, I orchestrated over 100 microservices while ensuring stability and maintainability. Automated checks and centralized documentation eliminated environment reliability issues, cementing a productive developer ecosystem.

Lessons Learned and Future Gains

As my journey unfolded, external challenges impacted InVision's trajectory. While our competitor gained an upper hand, the lessons learned during my tenure have profoundly impacted my future engineering decisions. Looking back, it is easy to see what myself and the company could have done differently to improve the outcome of InVision's products and the company itself.

CoursePark

Built in 2008

I exclusively worked on CoursePark while employed at Bluedrop. CoursePark was little more than a thought scribbled on paper when we started development and has since become a cornerstone product from Bluedrop with 10s of thousands of active users.

About a year into development I was promoted to Lead Programmer of CoursePark and my team and I continued to improve and implement the latest technologies such as the newly established eLearning API called TinCan. We also expanded to a mobile offering. As Lead on the project, I pushed us towards a Continuous Deployment model utilizing Unit & Functional Tests along with Pull Request style peer review. We also migrated from using SVN to GIT for version control which showed substantial performance improvements and allowed us to obtain the rapid development and release workflow we needed.

We built CoursePark on PHP, Postgres and the Zend Framework using the MVC architecture pattern. Our servers have been architected to provide a reliable uptime, performance and avoid any single point of failure. Vagrant VMs were used for local development along with Puppet scripts for managing development and production configurations. All of this was the latest technology at the time.

Update: After many years, CoursePark is finally shutting down as of August 2023
